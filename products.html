Vegetables

Beetroot is the root portion of the beet plant. They are deep purple in color and are eaten raw, boiled or fried and used as a seasoning in salads, ice creams. Beetroot juice can lower blood pressure, increase blood flow and improves athletic performance.

Fresh Fruits

Mangoes are seasonal fruits. Mangoes are known as “King of Fruits”. They are juicy, sweet and add a tangy taste to various sauces and pickles. Mangoes are cultivated in frost free tropical and warmer subtropical areas. They are widely used in food industries to make beverages, jams.

Spices

Black pepper or Piper nigrum is originated from the family of piperceae, which is a dried spice and cultivated fruit. There are various health benefits offered by black pepper such as cold, cough, constipation, indigestion, and respiratory disorders.

Oil Seeds

Castor-oil- plant or Ricinus Communis is a perennial plant from spurge family. Castor oil is produced from castor seeds. These seeds have 40%- 60% of the oil that is rich in triglycerides. These seeds also contain ricin a water soluble toxin which is effective in birth control.

Cereals

Pearl millet or Bajra is grown in Africa, India subcontinent and is widely type of millet. Pearl millet has the largest kernels of all types of millet. Pearls millet is largely produced in India. Bajra is highly consumed in India and is referred as poor man food.

Pulses

Chana dal was also known as split Bengal gram is considered as a most important staple food in many countries. These are highly nutritious and easily digested. They play an important part role in many Indian, Pakistani, and Bangladeshi cuisines.
